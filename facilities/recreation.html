Recreation and culture in Wong Tai Sin

Although Wong Tai Sin is an old district, its urban planning is not as poor as other old areas in Hong Kong. There are quite a number of recreational and cultural facilities for residents here to spend their leisure time.

Wong Tai Sin Temple

Wong Tai Sin Temple is a landmark Taoist temple in Wong Tai Sin district for worshipping "Wong Tai Sin" (meaning of the Great Immortal Wong) built in 1921. However, on top of the Taoist elements, there are also Confucian and Buddhist components in the temple. The temple is administered by Sik Sik Yuen, a religious charity group in Hong Kong established when Wong Tai Sin Temple was built.

The temple is located just next to Wong Tai Sin MTR station. Perhaps due to its convenience, it attracts many people to visit especially in the Chinese New Year for good fortune in the new year. In particular, a big crowd waited to offer the "first incense" to the "gods" inside the temple at the time when the new year comes.

Morse Park

Morse Park is one of the major public parks in the Wong Tai Sin district. It consists of four separate parks, an indoor sports center and several swimming pools. There are numerous fields for basketball and football (OKAY, it is called soccer in North America!). The park is close to both Wong Tai Sin and Lok Fu MTR stations.

Morse Park was involved in Hong Kong politics! Police bribery was very serious in Hong Kong. The grievances heightened in 1973 when Peter Godber, a chief superintendent fled to the UK when he faced bribery accusations. Mass protests, illegal and rarely seen at that time, were held and one of them was in Morse Park. The Independent Commission Against Corruption (ICAC) was eventually set up in 1974 to combat corruptions.

Lion Rock Park

Lion Rock Park is a park on the Lion Rock, a landmark mountain in Hong Kong. The park is near Chuk Yuen area of the district. There are simple playground facilities, but most people go there for a barbecue.

Do not mix the park with Lion Rock Country Park, which is managed under a stricter set of laws (for environmental conservation) and a different government department! Lion Rock Park is just next to this country park, where people can enjoy hiking - many like to walk across the mountain from Wong Tai Sin to Shatin, another district in the New Territories of Hong Kong.

Hammer Hill Road Sports Ground

Hammer Hill Road Sports Ground is said to be located at Diamond Hill, but it is actually just next to Choi Hung MTR station. It is one of the few sports ground with standard track and field areas. When I was a teacher, I visited there several times during my serving school's athletics' meets!

Just next to the sports ground, there is several swimming pools. One of them is indoor and provides warm water, which makes it very popular in winter.
